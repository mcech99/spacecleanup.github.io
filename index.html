Policy

The policy to aid in the clean-up of space debris will be added to the Outer Space Treaty. This policy is two-fold -- private and internatiaonal. Currently, any debris owned by a certain organization cannot legally be altered or moved by any other organization. That policy will be changed so that any un-identifiable piece of debris will be put under the jurisdiction of the International Space Station for clean-up. The organizations behind the ISS (NASA, CSA, ESA, Roscosmos, and JAXA) will lead and aid in the clean-up of general debris, which includes the un-identifiable debris. These organizations will contract the International Space Junkies company to retrieve the debris using net and harpoon technology.
The identifiable debris (i.e. nonfunctional satellites) must be retrieved by their respective organizations within 7 years of the no longer functioning, or otherwise receive heavy fines. The organziations also must submit a plan for retrieval before receiving approval from their respective organization (FAA for the United States) to send anything up into space. 

Contractor

Currently, the International Space Station and private companies can contract the International Space Junkies (ISJ) to retrieve debris. Contracting ISJ will allow for a more sustainable clean-up project as ISJ will be able to carry out more than one clean-up mission at a time. We hope as this policy is put into place, more companies will form with the same goal in mind, aiding in a faster clean-up process and creating a more competitive market aimed at sustainability.
